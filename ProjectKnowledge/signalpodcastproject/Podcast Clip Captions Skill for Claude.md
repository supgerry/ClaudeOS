# Podcast Clip Captions

Generate scroll-stopping titles and captions for short podcast video clips that match Signal's brand voice.

## Required Inputs

1. **Transcript** of the video clip
2. **Episode context** (guest name, title, company, episode topic, episode link)
3. **Target platforms** (LinkedIn, YouTube, or both)

## Output Format

For each clip, produce:

```
## Video Title
[6-12 word title that captures the hook]

## LinkedIn Caption
[1-2 line caption with emoji, ending with engagement prompt or call-to-action]

## YouTube Caption
[1-line caption, slightly adapted for YouTube's context]

Full episode: [YouTube link]
```

## Process

1. **Identify the hook**: Find the most engaging insight, counterintuitive claim, or actionable takeaway
2. **Extract the core tension**: What problem is being solved? What assumption is being challenged?
3. **Write the title**: Make it specific and benefit-driven, not generic
4. **Craft the caption**: Spark curiosity, invite engagement, ground it in what was said

## Signal Brand Voice

- **Professional yet approachable**: Expert insights without jargon
- **Curiosity-driven**: Lead with questions or surprising insights
- **Action-oriented**: Focus on what operators can learn or do
- **Authentic**: Ground copy in actual transcript contentâ€”never invent claims

## Title Guidelines

**Do:**

- Use 6-12 words
- Lead with the hook or transformation
- Include specific details when impactful (names, metrics, frameworks)
- Use title case

**Don't:**

- Be vague or generic ("Great Advice on Leadership")
- Use clickbait that doesn't match content
- Exceed 12 words

**Strong patterns:**

- "How [Company] [Achieved Result]"
- "Why [Common Belief] [Is Wrong/Incomplete]"
- "[Framework/Concept]: [Specific Application]"
- "The [Metric/Approach] That [Changed Outcome]"

## Caption Guidelines

**LinkedIn:**

- 1-2 lines maximum for the hook
- Include 1-2 relevant emojis (not excessive)
- End with engagement prompt (question, "ğŸ‘‡", or "here's how")
- Tag guest with proper title formatting
- Include both YouTube link and episode page link

**YouTube:**

- Single line, slightly more direct
- No emojis required (optional)
- Include full episode link
